Welcome to my website!

I am a postdoctoral researcher at the Shanghai Astronomical Observatory (SHAO), Chinese Academy of Sciences, working with Prof. Tie Liu and Prof. Xing Lu. I received my Ph.D. in Astrophysics from Peking University (PKU) in 2024, supervised by Prof. Ke Wang. During my PhD, I was also a guest researcher at the Max Planck Institute for Astronomy (MPIA) in Heidelberg, Germany, working with Prof. Henrik Beuther.

My research focuses on the early stages of high-mass star formation. Using high-resolution interferometric observations with facilities such as ALMA, SMA, and NOEMA, I aim to understand the physical, dynamical, and chemical processes that regulate the formation of massive stars. In particular, I investigate the structure and evolution of dense clumps and cores, the role of magnetic fields and multi-scale gas flows, and the fragmentation and chemical evolution in deeply embedded star-forming environments.
